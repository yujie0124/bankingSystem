BANK SYSTEM

Banking refers to any business entity that accepts and safeguards the finances of other persons and organizations and lends the finances to carry out economic activities such as making profits. Various types of bank accounts exist to attend to the different needs of the consumers. The following are the different types of banking accounts and the purpose we serve.

  • Savings accounts:

    This is used to set aside finances for future use. Most of the saving accounts pay interest on the deposits over time and have no debit or credit cards.

  • Goals accounts:

    Goal Accounts help to save for specific upcoming expenses such as a vacation or a new car. Goal Accounts automatically deduct a set amount from each paycheck to keep budget on track.

  • Investments accounts:

    An investment account is a current account linked to a securities account. It is used to transfer money in transactions to securities and deposit services.
